Welcome! My name is Jing Qian, and I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Politics at Princeton University. In my dissertation, I examine explores the domestic and international politics of international taxation, with a focus on why governments have been reluctant and unable to curb tax avoidance for decades. More broadly, I study the domestic and international politics of public finance. Some of my work has been published in International Organization and Public Administration.

Before coming to Princeton, I received a B.A. in international politics and an M.A. in international public policy from Fudan University, Shanghai, China.

Research Interests

International taxation, multinational corporations, foreign direct investment, foreign aid, multilateral development banks, quantitative methods.
